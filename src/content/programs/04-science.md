Students use AI-powered simulations to run experiments their schools can't
afford the equipment for — modelling projectile motion, circuits, and reactions
— and use image-recognition tools to understand how machines "perceive" the
world. They also learn where AI models break down, where simulation diverges
from reality, which is real scientific thinking.
