Students use AI to make sense of their own city's economy. They analyse real
Hong Kong data — housing costs, wages, inequality — with AI helping them query
and visualise it, turning abstract ideas like supply and demand, opportunity
cost, and the wealth gap into questions about their own neighbourhoods. They run
AI-assisted simulations to see how prices and incentives move.
